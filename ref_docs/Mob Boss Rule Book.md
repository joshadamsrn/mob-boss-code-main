## **Overview**

Mob Boss is a live‑action social deduction game for 7–25 players. Players move freely through a shared space, form alliances, trade items, commit murders, and participate in trials. The game is overseen by a moderator and is assisted by an app.

Players belong to one of three groups:

* **Police** (team-based)  
* **Mob** (team-based)  
* **Merchants** (independent, solo win condition)

The Police and Mob compete to eliminate each other, while Merchants attempt to reach a personal money goal to win.

---

## **The Moderator**

The Moderator is responsible for running the game fairly and smoothly.

Moderator responsibilities include:

* Managing the moderator shop  
* Confirming deaths and eliminations  
* Overseeing trials and jury selection  
* Managing item stashes and secret item transfers  
* Enforcing rules and resolving disputes  
* Maintaining the game’s atmosphere

The Moderator is the final authority on all rule interpretations.

**Merchants**

Merchants are **independent players** and do not belong to either team.

Merchant rules:

* Each Merchant has a personal money goal required to win  
* Merchants start the game by purchasing items from the Moderator Shop before the game begins  
* Merchants may purchase additional items during gameplay **except during trials**  
* Merchants earn money by any means possible which could include:  
  * Selling weapons and items  
  * Making deals with players  
  * Murdering players

### **Item Liquidation**

At the end of the game, Merchants may sell any remaining items back to the Moderator Shop for the **same price they were purchased for**. The value is added to their final money total.

## **Mob**

The Mob works as a hidden team attempting to eliminate the Police.

Mob rules:

* The Mob begins the game with a **secret code word** used to identify each other through conversation  
* Mob members may commit murders  
* Mob members may trade, steal, or kill to acquire items  
* The Mob wins when all Police are eliminated

### **Knife Hobo**

* The Knife Hobo is part of the Mob  
* Starts with **one knife (washable marker)** and **$0**  
* Always lowest rank in the Mob hierarchy

---

**Trials**

* A trial begins when a player is killed  
* The **Police Chief selects one player** to put on trial  
* The Moderator randomly selects a jury  
* The jury votes **Innocent** or **Guilty**

### **Trial Outcomes**

* **Guilty**: Player is eliminated from the game  
* **Innocent**: Game immediately advances to the next round

Trials may not be interrupted, and no purchases or murders may occur during trials

## **Police**

The Police work together to eliminate the Mob using limited force and control of the justice system.

Police rules:

* Police begin the game with more money than the Mob  
* Police may commit a **limited number of kills**  
* Exceeding the kill limit results in an automatic Police loss  
* The Police win when all Mob members are eliminated

### **Police Chief**

* Police Chief has the ability to choose who goes on trail  
* Police Chief starts out with the most money in the game  
* Police Chief is the highest rank in the Police hierarchy

---

## **Winning the Game**

**Mob Victory:** All Police are eliminated

**Police Victory**: All Mob members are eliminated (without exceeding kill limit)

**Merchant Victory**: A Merchant reaches their personal money goal

## **Hierarchy & Succession**

### **Police Hierarchy**

1. Police Chief  
2. Deputy  
3. Police Officer 1  
4. Police Officer 2 (and so on)

If the Police Chief is eliminated, the **Deputy immediately becomes the new Police Chief**. The hierarchy then shifts upward.

### **Mob Hierarchy**

1. Mob Boss  
2. Mob Member 1  
3. Mob Member 2 (and so on)  
4. Knife Hobo (always lowest rank)

If the Mob Boss is eliminated, the **next Mob Member in line becomes the new Mob Boss**.

---

**Murders & Deaths**

### **Honor System**

* Murders operate on an **honor system**  
* If you are killed, you must immediately admit your death to the moderator

### **Death Rules**

* Once killed or eliminated via trial:  
  * You may not speak  
  * You may not communicate information to living players  
  * You may not influence the game in any way

## **Item Transfer After Murders**

* If a player successfully murders another player and is **not eliminated via trial**, they gain **all items and money** from the victim

### **Trial-Based Item Redistribution**

* If the **Police Chief correctly identifies the killer** and has them eliminated via trial:  
  * All items gained from the murder **and** the killer’s original items are transferred to the **Police Chief**  
* If the **Police Chief accuses the wrong person** and that person is eliminated:  
  * All items belonging to the eliminated player are transferred to the **Mob Boss**

### **Item Transfer Method**

* The Moderator creates a **secret stash location**  
* The Police Chief or Mob Boss retrieves items privately  
* Once the items are set at the stash location it is free game if anyone else finds them  
* The same stash system is used for:  
  * Merchant starting items

## **Weapons**

Weapons are used to eliminate players and influence the flow of the game. All weapon use operates on an **honor system**.

### **Knives**

Knives are melee weapons purchased through Merchants or the Moderator Shop (If you are a merchant).

* Knives are represented by **washable markers**  
* A valid knife kill is a visible mark on a player’s **skin**  
* Players may **not mark clothing**  
* If marked by a knife, the player must immediately admit their death

### **Guns**

Guns are ranged weapons purchased through Merchants or the Moderator Shop.Guns are divided into three tiers:

* **Tier I Gun**: Basic firearm  
* **Tier II Gun**: Mid-level firearm  
* **Tier III Gun**: High-level firearm

(All gun tiers function as lethal weapons unless otherwise specified by the Moderator.)

If a player is shot by a gun, they must **immediately admit their death**, unless protected by a valid defensive item.

## **Special Items**

Special items are **one-time use items** that provide powerful effects.

### **Bulletproof Vest**

* One-time use defensive item  
* Protects the wearer from **one gunshot only**  
* Does **not** protect against knives  
* When a bulletproof vest is used, the wearer survives  
* The **Moderator announces** that a Bulletproof Vest was used  
* A **trial still begins** for attempted murder  
* The vest is then discarded and cannot be used again

  ### **Get Out of Jail Free Card (GOJFC)**

* One-time use item  
* May be used **after being voted Guilty** during a trial  
* When used, the player is immediately freed and remains in the game  
* The trial ends, and the next round begins  
* The GOJFC is discarded after use

**Money Redistribution:**

* If the player **was guilty** and used the GOJFC, the money spent on the card goes to the **Police Chief**  
* If the player **was innocent** and used the GOJFC, the money spent on the card goes to the **Mob Boss**  
* The money spent on the Bulletproof Vest is **refunded to the player** who used it
